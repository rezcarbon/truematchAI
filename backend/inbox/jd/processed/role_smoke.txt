Director (Ecosystem Integration), National AI Group — Ministry of Digital Development and Information (MDDI), Singapore.

Context: Singapore's National AI Strategy 2.0 sets the direction for how AI is developed and adopted across economy and public sector. Realising it depends on a vibrant, well-connected AI community where researchers, startups, large companies, government agencies, and overseas talent meet often enough to find common purpose and act. Reporting to the Chief AI Officer, the Director (Ecosystem Integration) sits within MDDI's National AI Group (NAIG) and is the STEWARD of that community. Brokers connections across government, industry, and research so shared use cases, research goals and missions surface and translate into joint work; anchors and grows Singapore's AI community-building hubs — Lorong AI today and Kampong AI as it comes online — as the places those connections form.

Working on:
- BROKER CONNECTIONS across the AI ecosystem: surface common purpose across government, industry, research (shared use cases, joint research goals, missions); convene the right parties. Connect AI talent with industry opportunities, research groups with applied problems, overseas talent with Singapore projects.
- STEWARD LORONG AI as the spiritual home of Singapore's AI community: lead programming (talks, founder dinners, technical meetups, hackathons, AI Wednesdays); curate and grow membership (researchers, founders, engineers, investors, policymakers); drive CO-LOCATION — get AI startups, research groups, AI-adjacent firms to locate offices/labs in the surrounding cluster so the community thickens physically.
- PROGRAMME KAMPONG AI: shape its programming concept as it comes online (tenant mix, shared-space design, activity rhythm, institutional anchors); translate Lorong AI lessons into a scalable programming model preserving character.
- ENGAGE THE ECOSYSTEM: sustain a wide active set of relationships across founders, researchers, engineers, corporate AI leads in Singapore and region; represent NAIG in community forums; be a key point of contact for ecosystem actors plugging into Singapore's AI agenda.

For someone who: believes communities are built deliberately (programming, membership curation, physical proximity); enjoys finding latent overlap between people on adjacent problems and getting them in a room; wants a hands-on role building the institutional fabric of Singapore's AI community at a formative moment.

What we're looking for:
- Background in a relevant field (Computer Science, Engineering, Public Policy, Business, or related discipline).
- At least 8–10 years of experience in technology, community building, ecosystem development, or related roles, with a track record of running substantive programming and convening across diverse groups.
- Strong understanding of AI technologies and the ecosystem dynamics that shape how AI communities form and grow.
- Demonstrated ability to broker connections and build trust across founders, researchers, and government counterparts.

- Familiarity with Singapore's digital and innovation ecosystem is preferred.
